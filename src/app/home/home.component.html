Ryan Bressette

I am a driven, results oriented software developer that loves using computers to solve complex problems. I have a stellar work ethic. I do not wait for managers to give me work; I am proactive in finding work to be done. I love learning new things especially when they are related to languages and encoding information. Programming provides the thrill of learning new things with each project and, once it is all over, I have an awesome project that I built myself. For me, that sense of accomplishment is the best feeling in the world.

I am currently finishing my Bachelor's Degree in Computer Science at Florida Polytechnic University in Lakeland, Florida. I will graduate May 3rd. I am moving to North Carolina to Wilmington or Cary depending on where I can find work. I am looking for a software development position to fill my love of learning new things and work on incredible projects to make a difference in the world.

About Me

I was born Leesburg Regional Medical Center and raised in Sumter County Florida. In Elementary school, I took part in Cub Scouts and a local soccer club. In Middle school, I was in 4H and FFA. In High school, I was in 4H, FFA, and National Honors Society. I was in the gifted program all 13 years that I was in public school.

In my 8 years in 4H, I mainly raised and showed a flock of Dorper sheep. I also did dog, steer, photography, and shooting sports projects. I did many hours of community service at 4H Camp Ocala in the Summer as a camp counselor while I was in highschool. My last year I volunteered at the local Boys and Girls Club teaching Python.

I graduated from South Sumter High School with honors in Spring 2016. I then went to Lake Sumter State College to get my AA degree. The AP and Dual Enrollment courses I took in high school allowed me to finish my AA in just one year. I was originally going to use the Direct Connect program to transfer to UCF for music education, but I found my love of programming shortly after I graduated high school, so I decided to pursue a Bachelor's of Science at Florida Polytechnic University instead. At Poly my love of computers and programming has grown tenfold and I am ecstatic to be entering the workforce soon and making a difference with my skills.
